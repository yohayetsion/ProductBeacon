## Design Philosophy: Why This Is NOT Product Outreach

ProductBeacon email outreach has three fundamental differences from product cold email (SKYMOD, Maad House):

1. **You are the product.** The buyer is evaluating whether to embed Yohay in their company 2-4 days/week. Every email must feel like it came from a senior peer, not a vendor. If it reads like marketing, it fails.

2. **Trust precedes the ask.** In product sales, you can lead with a demo or free trial. In high-ticket consulting, you lead with proof that you understand their world better than they expect. The CTA is never "buy." It is always "let's talk about your situation."

3. **Volume is the enemy.** SKYMOD sends 50 T1s/day. ProductBeacon should send 5-10/week, each deeply researched. A bad cold email from a watch brand loses a sale. A bad cold email from a fractional CPO candidate loses all credibility with that buyer forever.

**The golden rule:** Every ProductBeacon email should pass this test: "Would I be comfortable if the recipient showed this to their board?"

## Sequence 1: Signal-Triggered Cold Outreach (T1-T4)

### Signal Detection (What Triggers Outreach)

Only reach out when there is a verifiable buying signal. Never "spray and pray."

| Signal | Where to Find It | Urgency | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|---------|-------|
| **New funding round** (Seed-B) | Crunchbase, LinkedIn, TechCrunch, Geektime | High | 2-4 weeks post-announcement is the sweet spot. They are hiring but roles are not yet filled. |
| **VP Product / CPO departure** | LinkedIn job changes, company announcements | High | The gap is felt immediately. Timing matters: reach out within 1-2 weeks. |
| **"Head of Product" job posting** | LinkedIn Jobs, company careers page | Medium | They know they need it. Offer a bridge while they search. |
| **Product reorg announcement** | LinkedIn, press, industry chatter | Medium | Often signals dissatisfaction with product direction. |
| **New CEO/CTO at a startup** | LinkedIn, press | Medium | New leadership often reassesses product strategy. |
| **Failed product launch / pivot** | Product Hunt, press, user reviews | Medium | They need someone who has navigated pivots before. |
| **Board change / new investor** | Crunchbase, LinkedIn | Low-Med | New board members often push for product discipline. |
| **Competitor raised a round** | Crunchbase | Low | Creates urgency to sharpen product strategy. |

### T1: Initial Outreach (Day 0)

**Objective:** Get a reply. Not a meeting. Not a sale. A reply.

**Timing:** Send Tuesday-Thursday, 08:00-10:00 local time (buyer's timezone).

**Length:** 4-6 sentences. Maximum 100 words of body text. Shorter than you think.

**Structure:**

```
Subject line: [Signal-specific, curiosity-driven, no pitch]

[One sentence acknowledging a specific, verifiable thing about their situation]

[One sentence connecting that to a pattern you have seen / a relevant insight]

[One sentence establishing credibility — ONE proof point, not your resume]

[The ask: low-friction, specific, peer-level]

[Sign-off: first name only]
```

**Subject Line Approaches:**

| Type | Example | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Signal-based question** | "Quick thought on [Company]'s product roadmap" | Post-funding, visible product challenges |
| **Observation** | "Noticed [Company] is hiring a Head of Product" | Job posting signal |
| **Pattern recognition** | "Something I've seen at companies post-Series A" | Post-funding, general |
| **Specific insight** | "Your competitor just raised $20M. Here's what that means." | Competitor funding |
| **Direct** | "Product leadership bridge while you hire" | VP/CPO departure |

**Subject Lines to NEVER Use:**

- "Can I get 15 minutes?" (begging)
- "Partnership opportunity" (vague, salesy)
- "How [Company] can improve its product strategy" (presumptuous)
- "[Company] + ProductBeacon" (nobody knows who you are yet)
- Anything with "quick question" (overused, manipulative)

**CTA Options (Low to Medium Friction):**

| CTA | Friction | When to Use |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| "Worth a conversation?" | Lowest | Default for first touch |
| "Happy to share what I've seen work in similar situations." | Low | When you have a relevant pattern |
| "I put together a short framework that might be useful. Want me to send it?" | Low | When you have relevant content |
| "Would a 20-minute diagnostic call be useful? No pitch, just pattern-matching." | Medium | When signal is very strong |

### T2: The Value-Add Follow-Up (Day 4-5)

**Objective:** Provide something useful, unprompted. Prove you are a giver, not a taker.

**Timing:** 4-5 business days after T1. Same time window.

**Length:** 3-5 sentences + a link or attachment.

**Strategy:** Do NOT repeat the pitch. Instead, share something genuinely useful that demonstrates expertise and relates to their situation. This is the "proof of work" touch.

**Content Options (pick one based on relevance):**

| Content Type | Example | Best For |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| **Relevant framework** | "Here's the framework I use for post-funding product strategy prioritization." | Post-funding signals |
| **Industry insight** | "I wrote up observations on how [their competitor] is positioning. Thought you might find it useful." | Competitive pressure |
| **Case study excerpt** | "We faced a similar challenge at AXIA Security. Here's what worked." | Pattern match to their situation |
| **Content piece** | Link to a relevant LinkedIn article or blog post you wrote | Any signal |
| **Specific observation** | "I looked at [Company]'s product page. Two things stood out to me." | Strong enough interest to research |

**Example T2:**

```
Subject: RE: Something I noticed about the Series A timing

Hi [First Name],

Following up — not to pitch, just thought this might be
useful.

I put together a short framework on the three product
decisions that post-Series A companies need to lock down
before they start hiring PMs. It's based on patterns from
my last few engagements.

[Link to framework / attached PDF]

No strings. If it sparks a question, happy to chat.

Yohay
```

**Rules for T2:**
- NEVER say "just following up" or "bumping this to the top"
- NEVER repeat the CTA from T1
- The value-add must be genuinely useful, not a thinly-veiled pitch
- If you cannot find something genuinely useful to share, skip T2 and go to T3

### T3: The Pattern-Match Touch (Day 12-14)

**Objective:** Create relevance by connecting their situation to a specific outcome you have delivered.

**Timing:** 12-14 business days after T1. This is the "last real try" — make it count.

**Length:** 4-7 sentences. Slightly longer than T1 because you are telling a brief story.

**Strategy:** Share a concise case study micro-narrative that maps directly to their situation. This is NOT a generic "we helped Company X." It is a specific parallel: "Company like yours, problem like yours, here is what happened."

**Example T3:**

```
Subject: How a startup in your position solved this

Hi [First Name],

Last note from me — I know your inbox is a warzone
post-funding.

A cybersecurity startup in a similar position came to
us with 40 features on the roadmap and no framework for
saying no. In 8 weeks, we narrowed that to 3 strategic
bets, built a competitive positioning framework, and
created an interactive demo that their sales team actually
used. Documented value: $106K.

The situation feels similar to what [Company] might be
navigating right now. If I'm wrong, no worries. If I'm
right, 20 minutes would be useful for both of us.

Yohay
```

**Rules for T3:**
- Acknowledge their time is valuable ("last note from me")
- The case study must genuinely map to their situation
- End with a graceful out ("if I'm wrong, no worries")
- This is your best shot — make the story specific and compelling

### T4: The Breakup / Long-Game Seed (Day 30)

**Objective:** Close the loop gracefully while planting a seed for future contact.

**Timing:** 30 calendar days after T1. This is the final touch.

**Length:** 2-3 sentences. Shortest email in the sequence.

**Strategy:** Be human. Acknowledge you did not connect. Leave the door open without pressure. Optionally, offer one last piece of value.

**Example T4:**

```
Subject: RE: Something I noticed about the Series A timing

Hi [First Name],

Clearly the timing isn't right, and I respect that.

If product leadership becomes a priority down the road,
I'm easy to find. In the meantime, I post frameworks
and case studies on LinkedIn — might be useful background
reading: [LinkedIn profile link].

All the best with the build.

Yohay
```

**Example T4 (alternative — with value offer):**

```
Subject: One last thing

Hi [First Name],

I'll stop filling your inbox. But before I do — I just
published an open-source product org operating system with
82 AI agents built for exactly the kind of product work
you're scaling into.

It's free, no strings: [GitHub link]

Rooting for [Company].

Yohay
```

**Rules for T4:**
- NEVER guilt-trip ("I've sent you three emails...")
- NEVER be passive-aggressive
- Keep it warm, brief, and genuinely respectful
- The goal is that if they need you in 6 months, they remember you as "that thoughtful person who was not pushy"

### Sequence Timing Summary

| Touch | Day | Objective | Tone | Length |
|-------|-----|-----------|------|--------|
| **T1** | 0 | Get a reply | Peer-to-peer, signal-specific | 4-6 sentences |
| **T2** | 4-5 | Provide value | Generous, no pitch | 3-5 sentences + content |
| **T3** | 12-14 | Pattern-match story | Compelling, specific | 4-7 sentences |
| **T4** | 30 | Graceful close | Warm, brief, door open | 2-3 sentences |

**Total sequence duration:** 30 days, 4 touches.

**If they reply at any point:** Exit the sequence immediately. Move to a personalized conversation. NEVER send the next automated touch after receiving a reply.

## Sequence 2: Content-Nurture Sequence

### Who Enters This Sequence

Prospects who:
- Replied to cold outreach but did not book a call
- Had a discovery call that did not convert to a proposal
- Engaged with content (liked 3+ LinkedIn posts, visited website) but did not reach out
- Were referred but the timing was not right
- Downloaded Product Org OS or starred the GitHub repo

**Entry trigger:** Manual placement. This is a curated list, not a mass drip.

### Cadence

**One email every 2 weeks for 12 weeks (6 emails total).** Then move to the monthly newsletter (Sequence 4, not covered here — that is a broader content distribution play, not an outreach sequence).

### Content Strategy Per Email

Every email must be PURE VALUE. No pitch. No CTA beyond "reply if you want to chat." The goal is to stay top-of-mind as a credible, generous expert.

| Email | Timing | Content Type | Subject Approach | Content Framework |
|-------|--------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **N1** | Week 1 | Framework / How-To | "The [framework name] I use with every new client" | Share an actionable framework from Vision to Value. Something they can use immediately without hiring you. |
| **N2** | Week 3 | Case Study Deep-Dive | "What happened when [type of company] had no product leader for 6 months" | Tell a story with specifics. Problem, what was tried, what worked, what did not. Make it feel like a conversation over coffee. |
| **N3** | Week 5 | Contrarian Insight | "[Common practice] is killing your product velocity" | Take a position. Challenge a common assumption in their world. Show you think differently and have the experience to back it up. |
| **N4** | Week 7 | Tool / Resource | "The open-source tool I built for exactly this" | Highlight a specific Product Org OS capability relevant to their situation. Link to GitHub. Make it useful, not promotional. |
| **N5** | Week 9 | Industry Analysis | "What I'm seeing across [their industry/stage] right now" | Synthesize patterns from your work and research. Position yourself as someone with a wide lens on their world. |
| **N6** | Week 12 | Direct Offer | "If the timing is better now" | Gentle re-engagement. Reference the original conversation. Offer a specific, low-friction next step (diagnostic call, strategy session, quick review of their roadmap). |

### Content-Nurture Email Format

```
Subject: [Curiosity-driven, not pitchy]

Hi [First Name],

[1-2 paragraphs of genuinely useful content — the kind
of thing they'd forward to their VP Product or co-founder]

[Optional: link to a deeper piece (blog post, LinkedIn
article, GitHub repo)]

[No CTA or a very soft one: "If this resonates, happy
to dig deeper on your specific situation."]

Yohay

P.S. [Optional: one-line humanizing note — a book
recommendation, a relevant industry observation,
something that makes you a person, not a sequence]
```

### Example N1 (Framework):

```
Subject: The 3 decisions that define post-Series A product strategy

Hi [First Name],

After our conversation last month, I kept thinking about
the prioritization challenge you mentioned. Here's the
framework I use with every company at your stage:

1. WHERE TO PLAY — Which market segment gets 80% of
   product investment? Most post-Series A companies try
   to serve 3-4 segments and serve none of them well.

2. HOW TO WIN — What is the one capability that, if you
   nail it, makes everything else easier? Not three
   things. One.

3. WHAT TO STOP — Which existing commitments should be
   killed or deprioritized? This is where most product
   leaders fail. Addition by subtraction.

I've written up the full framework with worksheets here:
[link]

No ask — just thought it might be useful given where
[Company] is right now.

Yohay

P.S. If you haven't read "Obviously Awesome" by April
Dunford, it pairs well with decision #2.
```

### Example N6 (Re-engagement):

```
Subject: Checking in on [Company]'s product build

Hi [First Name],

It's been about three months since we last talked. I've
been following [Company]'s progress — [specific genuine
observation, e.g., "the new pricing page looks sharp" or
"congrats on the partnership with X"].

If the product leadership question is still open, I've
got bandwidth for one new engagement starting [month]. I
could do a quick 30-minute diagnostic on where your
product org stands today — no commitment, just an honest
read from someone who's built and rebuilt product orgs
for 17 years.

Either way, rooting for you.

Yohay
```

### Rules for Content-Nurture:
- NEVER send more than 1 email per 2 weeks
- NEVER include a hard CTA until N6
- Every email must be genuinely useful as standalone content
- Personalize the opening line to reference your prior interaction
- If they reply at any point, exit the sequence and have a real conversation
- If they unsubscribe or ask to stop, honor it immediately and permanently

### Referral Thank-You Protocol

When someone makes a referral (whether it converts or not):

1. **Same-day acknowledgment:** Personal email or message thanking them
2. **Status update at 2 weeks:** Let them know what happened ("we had a great conversation" or "it wasn't the right fit, but I appreciate the intro")
3. **If it converts:** Send a handwritten note or a thoughtful small gift. Not a referral fee. A genuine thank-you.
4. **Reciprocate:** Within 30 days, find a way to refer business or opportunity back to them

## Sequence 4: Tone and Style Guidelines

### How ProductBeacon Emails Sound vs. Other Etsion Brands

| Dimension | SKYMOD | Maad House | ProductBeacon |
|-----------|--------|------------|---------------|
| **Formality** | Professional-casual | Professional-creative | Peer-to-peer executive |
| **Length** | Medium (150-250 words) | Medium (150-250 words) | Short (50-120 words for cold, 150-200 for nurture) |
| **Personalization** | Company-specific | Agency-specific with pitch deck | Deeply personal, signal-specific |
| **Volume** | 50/day | 50/day | 5-10/week |
| **CTA** | Meeting/demo | Meeting/portfolio review | Conversation/diagnostic call |
| **Tone** | B2B product pitch | Creative partnership pitch | Senior colleague reaching out |
| **HTML** | Branded template | Branded template with embedded deck | Plain text. No HTML. No images. |
| **Sender** | yohay@skymod.watch | yohay@maad.house | yohay@productbeacon.agency |

### The ProductBeacon Email Voice

**Sounds like:** A senior product leader writing from their phone between meetings. Someone who is busy, successful, and genuinely interested in your problem. Not selling. Sharing.

**Does NOT sound like:** A consulting firm's outbound program. A startup's growth hack email. An SDR running a Salesloft sequence. A LinkedIn influencer.

### Writing Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

1. **Plain text only.** No HTML templates, no images, no branded headers, no logos. High-ticket consulting emails that look like marketing get deleted. Plain text signals "this was written by a real person."

2. **Short paragraphs.** Never more than 3 lines per paragraph. White space is trust.

3. **No em dashes.** Per Etsion Brands outreach standards. Use periods, commas, colons, semicolons.

4. **First name sign-off.** Just "Yohay." Not "Yohay Etsion" or "Yohay Etsion, Founder, ProductBeacon" with a 10-line signature block. You are a person, not a company.

5. **One proof point per email.** NEVER list your full resume. Pick the ONE credential most relevant to their situation. "17 years at NICE/Cognyte" OR "$106K value in 8 weeks" OR "82 AI agents." Not all three in one email.

6. **Ask, don't tell.** "Worth a conversation?" beats "I'd love to set up a call to discuss how we can help." Questions invite dialogue. Statements invite deletion.

7. **No superlatives.** Never say "best-in-class," "world-class," "cutting-edge," "leading." Let the specifics speak.

8. **Respect the no.** If someone does not reply after T4, they go to the nurture list or they go silent. NEVER re-enter them into the cold sequence.

9. **No tracking pixels.** Controversial, but important for trust. If someone finds a tracking pixel in your email, you lose all credibility as a peer. Use reply rate as your primary metric, not open rate.

10. **Subject lines: lowercase or sentence case.** Never ALL CAPS. Never clickbait. "Quick thought on your product roadmap" or "bridging the product gap" — not "YOU NEED A CPO NOW."

### What to NEVER Do in High-Ticket Consulting Outreach

| Never Do | Why | What to Do Instead |
|----------|-----|-------------------|
| Send HTML templates with branding | Screams "mass email" | Plain text, personal tone |
| Use urgency ("limited availability") | Manipulative at this price point | State genuine availability if relevant |
| Name-drop other clients without permission | Breaks trust | Use anonymized descriptions ("a Series B cybersecurity startup") |
| Follow up more than 4 times on cold | Desperation signals | 4 touches max, then nurture or silence |
| Pitch on the first email | Premature | Lead with insight, observation, or pattern |
| Send identical emails to multiple people at the same company | Embarrassing when they compare notes | One contact per company, the most senior |
| Use "we" when it is one person | Deceptive scale signaling | "I" is honest and builds trust |
| Include pricing in cold outreach | Commoditizes before establishing value | Pricing comes in proposals, after diagnosis |
| Attach large decks or case studies unsolicited | Overwhelming and presumptuous | Offer to share, or link to a short piece |
| Auto-schedule follow-ups without reading their reply | Shows you are running a sequence, not having a conversation | Pause automation the moment they engage |

## Implementation Notes

### Send Infrastructure

- **Sender:** yohay@productbeacon.agency (set up in Phase 0)
- **No mass email tools.** Do not use Mailchimp, HubSpot sequences, or similar for cold outreach. Send individually from the actual email account. Tools are acceptable for the content-nurture sequence only, and only if they send from the real address with no tracking pixels.
- **CRM tracking:** Log every touch in a simple pipeline (spreadsheet or Notion for Phase 1). Track: prospect name, company, signal, T1 date, T2 date, T3 date, T4 date, reply (Y/N), outcome.

### Volume Guidelines

| Phase | Cold Outreach (T1s) | Nurture Emails | Referral Touches |
|-------|---------------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Phase 1 (Weeks 3-8)** | 5-10/week | 0 (building the list) | 5-10/week |
| **Phase 2 (Weeks 8-24)** | 5-10/week | 3-5/week (nurture list growing) | 2-3/week |
| **Phase 3 (Months 6-12)** | 5-10/week | 5-10/week | 2-3/week |

### Research Time Per Prospect (Cold)

Budget 20-30 minutes of research per T1 email:
- 5 min: LinkedIn profile (buyer's background, career, recent posts)
- 5 min: Company overview (stage, funding, team size, product)
- 5 min: Signal verification (confirm the trigger is real and current)
- 5 min: Competitive/market context (what challenges they likely face)
- 5-10 min: Writing and editing the email

This is NOT scalable to 50/day. It is not meant to be. Quality over quantity is the entire strategy.

### Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Reply rate (cold)** | >15% | Proves the emails feel personal and relevant |
| **Positive reply rate (cold)** | >8% | Proves the value prop resonates |
| **Discovery call from cold** | >5% | Proves the sequence converts |
| **Nurture-to-conversation rate** | >10% over 12 weeks | Proves the content keeps you top of mind |
| **Referral intro rate** | >20% of asks | Proves the network values you enough to vouch |
| **Time-to-reply** | <48 hours for positive replies | Confirms urgency alignment |
