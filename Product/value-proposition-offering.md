## 2. Service Architecture

### Design Rationale: Three Tiers, Not Four

The original four-tier model included "AI-Augmented Product Services" as a standalone tier. After analysis, this is a horizontal capability, not a separate offering. AI augmentation is baked into everything ProductBeacon does. Calling it a separate tier:
- Confuses buyers ("What's the difference between Tier 1 and Tier 4?")
- Positions AI as optional rather than core
- Creates pricing ambiguity

**The new structure uses three tiers**, each with AI augmentation built in. This is cleaner for buyers and more honest about the delivery model.

---

## 3. Engagement Models

### How Engagements Start: ProductBeacon Discovery

Every engagement begins with Discovery, a short, structured assessment that establishes fit, scope, and a recommended engagement plan. Discovery is not free consulting; it is a focused diagnostic that delivers immediate value.

**ProductBeacon Discovery (1-2 weeks)**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Format** | 3-5 working sessions (virtual), stakeholder interviews, document review |
| **Output** | Discovery Report: current state assessment, key opportunities, recommended engagement plan with scope, timeline, and deliverables |
| **Investment** | Paid (applied as credit toward the first engagement month if the client proceeds) |
| **Duration** | 1-2 weeks |

**Discovery covers**:
1. **Situation Assessment**: Where is the company today? What's the product, market, team, and competitive situation?
2. **Gap Analysis**: What's missing? Product strategy, roadmap, processes, team capability, GTM alignment?
3. **Opportunity Mapping**: What are the highest-impact opportunities for ProductBeacon to deliver value?
4. **Engagement Recommendation**: Proposed tier, scope, timeline, deliverables, and investment

**Why Discovery exists**:
- Ensures fit before commitment (for both sides)
- Delivers immediate value (companies learn something useful even if they don't proceed)
- Scopes the engagement accurately (prevents both under-delivery and scope creep)
- Builds trust through demonstrated competence

**Alternative entry point: Product Org Assessment (Tier 3)**

For companies specifically interested in Tier 3 (Product Org Services), Discovery takes the form of a more structured Product Org Assessment:
- Maturity assessment across 8 product dimensions
- Benchmarking against industry standards
- Prioritized transformation roadmap
- Detailed engagement plan

### How AI Changes the Value Equation

**Traditional Consulting Math**:
- Senior partner (sells, checks in occasionally): $800-$1,200/hour
- Manager (coordinates): $400-$600/hour
- Junior consultants (do the work): $200-$400/hour x 3-5 people
- Total for a strategic package: $200K-$500K+ over 3-6 months

**ProductBeacon Math**:
- One senior leader (sells AND does the work): Full engagement
- 82 AI agents (research, draft, analyze, iterate): Built into every hour
- Result: Team-level output, senior-level quality, fractional cost

**The specific AI advantages**:

1. **Research at scale**: AI agents can analyze competitors, markets, and trends in hours, not weeks. The human judgment decides what matters; the AI does the exhaustive groundwork.

2. **First-draft acceleration**: AI produces high-quality first drafts of PRDs, competitive analyses, positioning statements, and GTM plans. The senior leader refines, contextualizes, and adds judgment. This inverts the traditional ratio (80% drafting, 20% refining becomes 20% directing, 80% refining).

3. **Consistency across deliverables**: Because all deliverables are produced through a unified system (Product Org OS), strategic choices propagate automatically. The market analysis informs the positioning, which informs the PRD, which informs the GTM. Nothing contradicts.

4. **Iteration velocity**: When the strategy changes (and it will), the entire deliverable set can be updated quickly. Traditional consulting locks you into the first draft because revision is expensive.

5. **Organizational memory**: Every decision, assumption, and piece of feedback is captured and cross-referenced. Six months into an engagement, the AI system remembers why a pricing decision was made and can surface it when a related topic comes up.
