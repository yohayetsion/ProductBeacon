Strategic Moat

Three layers of defensible advantage

01

Spatial Legal Intelligence

The only platform that connects title deeds, historic conveyances, and environmental risk maps to the same piece of land. Generic legal AI is text-in, text-out. Orbital resolves documents to physical reality — that is a categorically different capability.

02

Data Integration Depth

Land Registry, historic deeds, OS maps, utility records, flood data — all resolved to a single spatial object. No competitor has this. Data providers surface raw data but don't synthesise it. Orbital turns a fragmented data landscape into a unified intelligence layer.

03

Domain-Trained AI at Scale

200,000+ transactions means Orbital's AI has trained on real property risk patterns across the full deal lifecycle. Generic legal AI has no equivalent corpus for property. Every new transaction deepens the moat — a compounding advantage that gets harder to replicate over time.

Recommendations

Four moves to lock in the moat

1

Lead with the category, not the clock

"The only platform that sees property as both law and land." Own spatial legal intelligence before a well-capitalised horizontal player — Harvey, Microsoft Copilot, or a resurgent LexisNexis — decides to claim it.

2

Make Visualise the hero

The map-based interface is visually distinctive and functionally unique across the entire legal tech market. It should be front and centre in demos, website, and sales collateral — not item two in a four-item list.

3

Localise US proof points

Seyfarth Shaw, Vinson & Elkins, Goodwin Procter should lead in US-facing materials. UK elite firms (Clifford Chance, Macfarlanes) carry significantly less weight with NYC real estate counsel making procurement decisions.

4

Productise certainty

Build a risk quantification layer into Report. Not "here are the issues" but "here is the residual risk score and confidence level." That's the language insurers like Zurich and lenders use — and it unlocks a second revenue motion beyond legal fees.

The Bigger Picture

A moat that compounds — if positioned to win

The spatial intelligence moat is real and getting deeper with every transaction. The question is whether the market sees Orbital as "fast legal AI" or "the only platform that understands property as a physical place."


The US expansion is the primary growth lever — no dominant specialist exists there yet. But well-capitalised horizontal players are moving into property workflows from above, and they move fast. Speed to a defensible US position matters more than perfection.


With dedicated product leadership shaping the category narrative, Orbital can transform from a fast tool into essential property intelligence infrastructure — the system of record for how land is understood, transacted, and de-risked across the global real estate market.
