PRODUCT STRATEGY BRIEF

You are building a workspace and selling a stopwatch.

A narrative read of Orbital's market, moat, positioning, and value proposition in the window between the Series B and Harvey's first real-estate vertical release.

THE MARKET

Two tailwinds, one timing window.

Orbital sits at the junction of two markets moving at different speeds. Legal AI is the hot capital magnet: US law firm tech spend grew 39.3% from 2021 to 2025 and average US firm profit rose 13.0% in 2025 (Thomson Reuters, 2026 State of the US Legal Market). The horizontal leaders have absorbed the oxygen, with CoCounsel acquired by Thomson Reuters for $650M and Harvey reported at roughly $5B valuation (LawNext, Spellbook).

The property diligence vertical underneath runs on different fuel. Real estate is framed by the CEO as "the world's largest asset class," with global transaction volume of roughly $1.5 to $2 trillion annually (JLL, CBRE) and legal fees at 0.5 to 2% of deal value. That implies $10 to $40 billion in annual real estate legal spend globally. Add UK specific force vectors: HMLR digitisation, the UK government home buying reform push, conveyancing backlog pressure, and SRA expectations around AI provenance.

YOUR MOAT

Three structural advantages. Not features.

01

Physical property grounding.

Mapping plus deeds plus documents tied to a specific parcel is an integration asset, not a feature. Every other legal AI reads text. Orbital reads where the title boundary meets the flood zone. Durability: high. A horizontal player has to buy spatial data partnerships, cartographic UI, and property entity resolution; it cannot ship them in a quarter.

02

Magic Circle reference depth compounding into procurement gravity.

A&O, Clifford Chance, Macfarlanes, V&E, Seyfarth, Cleary, BCLP on one logo wall is the procurement shortcut that lets mid-market firms skip their own evaluation. Durability: medium-high, time-bounded. Erodes if Harvey lands three Magic Circle real estate wins.

03

Jurisdictional topology baked into product.

UK Residential, UK Copilot, US Copilot, across firms, in-house, insurers, conveyancers. This is regulatory knowledge encoded in product surface, not in a prompt. Durability: high. Each new jurisdiction deepens the moat and raises the horizontal entrant's cost.

POSITIONING SCORECARD

The headline sells the mechanism, not the outcome.

"The Fastest Way To Reveal Real Estate Risk."

Orbital helps lawyers and property professionals get an immediate understanding of real estate matters, using trusted data, mapping and purpose-built AI.
BClarity. Concrete, reads in one breath, a non-specialist understands what the product does.
CDifferentiation. "Fastest" is a commodity claim in 2026. Every AI vendor claims speed. It invites a counter.
B+Believability. The 200,000 transactions per year number and the Magic Circle logo wall carry the proof.

The buried truth.

The Series B press release already framed it: "a workspace for real estate legal across the lifecycle of the entire real estate asset class." That line is the real product. The homepage buries it under a speed claim that the junior associate uses and the partner does not sign for.

Performance claims are inherently temporary and benchmarkable. Category claims are defensible. Orbital has the latter and is selling the former.

VALUE PROP GAP

Speed is the feature the associate uses. It is not what the partner signs for.

CURRENT "The Fastest Way To Reveal Real Estate Risk." Pillar one: "Deliver Outstanding Results to Clients Every Time." Mechanism foregrounded. Outcome implied.
REFRAMED "The real estate workspace that makes title and lease risk defensible, insurable, and client-ready on every matter, across every jurisdiction."

Outcomes foregrounded: defensible diligence (partner signoff ready, SRA auditable), insurability (reduces PI exposure as climate risk and bar ethics opinions tighten), portfolio scalability (the next hundred matters without the next hundred associates). Speed becomes an attribute of the workspace, not the category claim.

The buyer shifts when the frame shifts. Speed sells to practice leads. Defensibility plus insurability plus auditability sells to the Managing Partner, the CFO, the Risk Committee, and the insurance broker, which is the renewal and expansion buyer. Orbital is priced and logoed for the partner room but messaged for the associate desk. The customer quote from Vinson & Elkins already knows this: "It's not replacing legal judgment, it helps us apply it more effectively for clients." That is the reframe hiding inside your own site.

NARRATIVE OPPORTUNITY

From a faster review tool to the standard of care in real estate law.

TODAY

"We make real estate diligence faster."

A review tool narrative. Useful. Benchmarkable. Beatable.

TOMORROW

"We make property risk knowable, defensible, and insurable across the lifecycle."

A category narrative. The workspace claim the Series B press release already made. The claim only Orbital can make, because only Orbital resolves title, deed, map, and matter to the same physical parcel.

The Series B window is the moment to claim this, not document it. The $60M, the NY office, the 2025 headcount doubling, and the Legalweek 2026 presence are all assets that lose value if the category claim ships late. Once a horizontal competitor ships a real estate vertical module, "fastest" becomes a feature comparison Orbital is on the wrong side of. "The real estate workspace" is a category claim the horizontals cannot counter without buying the spatial layer.

RECOMMENDATIONS

Four product organization moves to close the workspace versus stopwatch gap in 90 days.

1

Retire the speed claim. Lead with the workspace claim.

Replace "The Fastest Way To Reveal Real Estate Risk" with a category claim grounded in the Series B narrative ("the real estate legal workspace") and in the one thing a horizontal competitor cannot ship (physical property grounding). Speed becomes a supporting attribute. Testable in A/B on the homepage and in outbound within three weeks.

2

Reframe the outcome ladder for the partner buyer.

Build a partner and GC facing outcome story around defensibility, insurability, auditability, and portfolio scalability. The current pillar language ("Deliver Outstanding Results") is internal sounding. The reframed ladder aligns to the Managing Partner, the Risk Committee, the CFO, and the insurance broker who sign renewals. This is the Director of Product Marketing's 90-day deliverable.

3

Collapse Witness, Copilot UK, and Copilot US into one product with jurisdiction as configuration.

Enterprise procurement is consolidating toward one AI vendor per firm. A biglaw buyer with both US and UK real estate practice should buy one product, not navigate three SKUs. Jurisdiction becomes a module, not a SKU. This is a VP Product decision, not a marketing one.

4

Move pricing from seat month to firm platform plus per matter consumption.

The value event is a matter closing, not a seat month. Seat pricing taxes light users and caps expansion inside firms where only a minority of lawyers touch property. A firm level platform fee plus per matter consumption aligns to the 200,000 transactions a year scale claim and opens the door to insurer and in-house billing models the seat license cannot. Owner: VP Product plus Finance.

THE BIGGER PICTURE

The window is a Series B window. It is measured in quarters, not years.

The horizontal versus vertical decision in legal AI will be made inside AmLaw 200 and UK mid-market procurement over the next 18 months. The firms that have not yet picked an AI vendor will pick one and stop evaluating. If Orbital is still described as a faster review tool when that evaluation happens, it loses seats to horizontals even though the vertical work is deeper. If Orbital is described as the real estate workspace with physical property grounding by the time that evaluation happens, it wins the seats a horizontal cannot backfill.

The asset is built. The story is behind the asset. Closing that gap is product leadership work: positioning, roadmap sequencing, SKU rationalization, pricing architecture. It is not a marketing campaign; it is the product organization owning the narrative the Series B already promised. That is the kind of work we do.
