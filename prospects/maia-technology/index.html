Structural Advantage

Your Moat

Three durable advantages — two architectural, one cultural — that competitors cannot replicate without rebuilding from scratch.

1
Real-Time Event-Driven IBOR
An architectural decision made at founding that incumbents cannot replicate without rebuilding their entire stack. End-of-day batch is a structural liability for managers running intraday strategies or operating across time zones. MAIA's positions are never stale — and that matters.
2
Streaming API for Systematic Managers
Quant managers want clean, low-latency position data piped directly into their models — not a vendor portal. MAIA's event-driven architecture makes streaming native, not bolted on. Python, Java, C#, and Go support signals that the API was designed for engineers, not afterthought.
3
Asset Management DNA
"Born out of an established asset management business with a decade of development." MAIA's data models reflect how PMs and ops teams actually work — not how engineers imagine they work. This is a trust asset that Bloomberg, Enfusion, and Limina cannot manufacture.

Value Proposition

Value Prop Gap

The gap between the current positioning and a differentiated alternative illustrates exactly what is being left on the table.

⚠️ Current — Amber
"Cloud-native technology delivers class-leading portfolio management through an open architecture"
Why it falls short: "Class-leading" is an unverified superlative. "Cloud-native" and "open architecture" are table stakes claimed by every modern PMS vendor. This sentence could be lifted from an Enfusion brochure without changing a word. It creates no preference and invites no conversation.
✓ Reframed — Green
"The investment platform built by people who ran portfolios, not people who watched them"
Why it works: Captures the asset management DNA advantage directly. Differentiates MAIA from Bloomberg (a data vendor), Enfusion (a pure-tech company), and Limina (a Nordics-first vendor). Implies better data models, better workflows, and fewer surprises. Invites the question "tell me more."
The real-time dimension can be layered in: "The investment platform built by practitioners — where your positions are never stale and your reconciliation happens in real time." Two differentiation axes, one sentence. Neither claim is made by any competitor.

Strategic Recommendations

Recommendations

Four focused moves that address the messaging gap without requiring product changes. The architecture is already there. The story is not.

1
Kill the carousel. Lead with real-time IBOR.
"Your positions are never stale. Your reconciliation happens in real time." This is where category pain is sharpest — batch IBOR is the most common complaint about incumbent platforms. Name the problem, name the fix, name the company that solved it architecturally. One headline. No carousel.
2
Build a quant/systematic developer portal.
API documentation, sandbox environment, Python and Go code examples. Systematic managers evaluate platforms by API doc quality before they request a demo. This is a low-cost, high-signal move that no other PMS vendor has executed well. MAIA's streaming API already exists — the portal just needs to surface it.
3
Surface the practitioner DNA above the fold.
"Born from an asset management business" is currently buried. It should be on the homepage, in the first 200 words, with specificity. How many years of internal development? What strategies were being run? The believability infrastructure is there — it just needs to be promoted from "About" to hero messaging.
4
Segment the website into distinct buyer pathways.
Emerging manager, systematic/quant, and established discretionary managers each have different evaluation criteria, different buying committees, and different primary pain points. One homepage message for all three means it resonates with none. Add "Start Here" pathways that fork by segment — a 2-week project with disproportionate conversion impact.
