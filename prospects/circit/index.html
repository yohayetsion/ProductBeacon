For decades, audit confirmations were paper-based and dependent on phone calls. PSD2 changes everything by enabling direct, bank-verified access to financial data. The incumbent (Capital Confirmation / Thomson Reuters) is architecturally disadvantaged.

A small window exists for an independent platform to establish itself as the standard before the Big Four build or acquire their own technology.

04
YOUR MOAT

Two Moats and a Hidden Platform

Regulatory Approval (AISP)

Account Information Service Provider approval from the Central Bank of Ireland and FCA. This is a multi-year regulatory process requiring dedicated compliance infrastructure and ongoing supervision. Every potential competitor must clear this threshold before accessing the same data.

Bank Integration Network

1,978 bank integrations, each a technical connection and a relationship built over years. A larger player could replicate this with deeper pockets, but the time cost is measured in years. Every new integration makes the platform more useful for every existing customer.

The Verified Data Layer

When an auditor pulls 100% of a client's bank transactions directly from the source, that dataset has value beyond audit: M&A due diligence, regulatory reporting, credit risk, fraud detection. Audit confirmations are the wedge. The verified data platform is the endgame.

THE NARRATIVE

From Five Products to One Data Story

TODAY

Circit tells the story of an auditor-founded company with five products and strong customer logos. The narrative is company-centric: who we are, what we built, who funds us. Five products are a flat list.

TOMORROW

Circit tells the story of what happens when audit evidence comes directly from the bank. Confirmation Requests is the hero. Verified Transactions extends the data layer. Collaboration and signing deepen the relationship. The story: "We give you verified financial data, then make everything you do with it faster."

08
RECOMMENDATIONS

Four Moves

  1. Restructure the Product Hierarchy

    Give Confirmation Requests the hero position. Build a land-and-expand narrative: enter with confirmations, expand into verified transactions, deepen with collaboration. Stop presenting five products as equals when one is the differentiator.

  2. Build the Open Banking Narrative

    Commission an industry report on the shift from self-reported to bank-verified audit evidence. Position Circit as the authority. Audit partners need language to justify adoption internally. Give them that language.

  3. Create Segment-Specific Messaging

    Mid-tier firms buy on efficiency (hours saved, faster turnaround). Big Four buy on strategic capability. Banks buy on ecosystem value. One message cannot serve all three.

  4. Address the Product Leadership Gap

    Five products at 55 people requires disciplined product management. Without a clear investment framework, engineering spreads thin and nothing reaches best-in-class depth. The absence of product leadership is the single biggest risk to the $22M investment.

09
THE OPPORTUNITY

The Bigger Picture

Circit has built something genuinely rare: a regulatory moat and a network moat in a market where the incumbent has not innovated in decades. The 1,978 bank integrations and PSD2 AISP approval would take years and significant capital for any new entrant to replicate.

But the castle these moats protect is spreading thin. Five products competing for attention, geographic expansion into different regulatory frameworks, and a leadership team that is commercially heavy with no visible product leadership.

Whether the company sees itself as an audit confirmation tool or a verified financial data platform will determine its ceiling, its competitive set, and whether the moats protect a narrow castle or a much larger territory.
