Product Strategy Brief

You are trying to out-shout UpToDate in a category they own.

A product-leader read of your positioning, moat, and the narrative you should be selling at Series A.

The Market

Clinical decision support is bifurcating, and you are on the right side of the split.

The broad CDS category runs roughly £1.7B in 2024 growing to £4.3B by 2030 at about 16% CAGR (Grand View Research, with similar ranges from Allied Market Research and MarketsandMarkets). That is not your wedge.

Your wedge is the narrower lane of local, institutional protocol management, where no clean analyst number exists and pure-play vendors are few. UK NHS dynamics are favourable: around 200 trusts across UK and Ireland, antibiotic stewardship and Care Quality Commission audit pressure driving procurement, junior doctors rotating every 4 to 6 months making local protocol access a real 3am friction. The NHS Long Term Plan digital maturity targets add tailwind. The US mirror market is larger but Epic-centric and fragmented.

Your Moat

Three compounding advantages, and none of them are "AI."

01

Trust-embedded deployment

40+ NHS trusts plus Ireland HSE, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland, with a US beachhead at Stanford, Mass General, Boston Children. Each trust has ingested local protocols, mapped them to departments, configured analytics, trained clinicians. NHS procurement cycles create 3 to 5 year lock-in once content is migrated.

Durability: very high
02

Consultant-curated local content

The value is not generic evidence. It is the IV monograph approved by Barts PICU pharmacy, the sepsis escalation path Imperial ED signed off. Human-curated authority that global reference tools structurally cannot produce. Governance sign-off makes it legally load-bearing.

Durability: high
03

Cross-professional analytics flywheel

Doctors, nurses, pharmacists, paramedics on one platform. Usage data converts from productivity metric to CQC audit evidence. Strongest in regulated UK and Ireland markets.

Durability: medium-high

These grades work for the clinical lead on a product demo. They fail for the trust CMO signing procurement and the CQC audit lead renewing the contract, and those are the two buyers who actually decide if you keep the account.

Value Prop Gap

Competing on speed of answer loses. Competing on a job nobody else can do wins.

Current
"The AI Answer Engine for Healthcare Teams."
Reframed
"The junior doctor on the night shift finds the approved escalation path in 20 seconds, not 20 minutes of phoning around."
"The trust can prove to CQC which clinicians read which protocol before an adverse event."

Why the gap matters: the current frame competes on speed of answer against tools with £100M+ brand equity. The reframe competes on a job nobody else can do, making local institutional decisions findable at 3am and audit-defensible the morning after. That one shift moves the buyer from clinical reference budget holder (crowded, commoditised, owned by librarians) to trust CMO and governance lead (uncontested, budgeted from quality-improvement lines, triggered by audit moments).

Narrative Opportunity

The £9M Series A opens a 12-month window to own the trust-level clinical standard.

Today

Chasing "AI Answer Engine"

Chasing the UpToDate and Dyna AI category on "AI answer engine" language. A race against £100M brands for a commodity claim, with your best proof points buried three scrolls down the page.

Tomorrow

Owning the trust-level standard

You are the only vendor that can credibly say "we are the operating system for your institution own protocols, defensible to CQC and the Joint Commission, usable by every clinician regardless of role." That is a category you already lead in the NHS and HSE, and it is uncontested.

US expansion is where "AI answer engine" collides head-on with UpToDate AI, and the only defensible ground is the local-protocol category you already own in NHS.

Recommendations

Four moves to make in the first 90 days post-raise.

1

Repivot the hero line within 90 days

Move from "The AI Answer Engine for Healthcare Teams" to an institution-first line naming the job no incumbent does. Shape: "Your trust clinical knowledge, searchable at the bedside." UpToDate cannot claim this. Dyna AI cannot claim this. The rewrite cascades into the trust-facing pitch, the pricing page, and the sales deck.

2

Reframe analytics from productivity to governance

"Monitor Advanced Analytics" today is a dashboard feature. Reframe as inspection-readiness proof: which clinicians read which protocol before which incident. That repackages the same data for a board-level buyer at a board-level price.

3

Build a CMO-grade SKU architecture

Per-clinician seats match a productivity tool. Your enterprise motion needs "trust group plus Integrated Care System" contracts tied to named governance outcomes, and a different US architecture (Epic-adjacent, academic medical centre land-and-expand, HIPAA-gated).

4

Ship the content-ops function you do not yet have

Stanford, MGH, GOSH, Barts is an enviable customer base. The blog has five thought-leadership posts, no full-length case studies, no release notes track, no event presence. A Series A healthtech with your logos should be running six case studies, a release cadence, and HIMSS, Rewired, NHS ConfedExpo presence.

The Bigger Picture

Series A is the moment founder-led product instinct stops scaling.

You have built something rare: a trust-embedded platform with 400+ institutional teams, a moat that compounds with every protocol ingested, and a category you already lead in two national health systems. What you do not yet have is the narrative discipline to defend that lead when US expansion puts you in front of buyers who have never heard of you and who default to the UpToDate line item they already pay.

Product leadership at this stage is not about adding features. It is about deciding which category you compete in, which buyer you sell to, and which SKU structure matches the £9M runway. Those decisions do not wait for the next hire cycle.
