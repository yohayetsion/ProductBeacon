Recommendations

Four moves, sequenced.

01

Re-anchor the homepage around the 10-minute vs 10-month asymmetry

Move "Unified Namespace" one click deeper for the architect buyer. Lead the hero with the outcome-and-speed claim backed by the quantified customer averages. The Handelsblatt quote becomes a proof element, not a banner. This single change converts earned media into budget-holder language.

02

Make the commercial wedge legible above the sales call

A plant IT director cannot tell from the site what the paid tier does that the free tier does not. That opacity throttles the bottoms-up motion the open-core model depends on. The boundary between community and commercial is a product-marketing artifact, not a sales-call disclosure. Publish it.

03

Productize the customer-proof layer

Hipp, Boellhoff, Sonnlaender, and Autoform are named references with quantified outcomes. Short-form testimonials undersell them. Long-form case studies with measurable outcomes feed sales enablement, analyst conversations, and the earned-media flywheel that already works for UMH.

04

Build the US positioning track as a separate product-marketing motion

The "gegen Microsoft und Siemens" framing is globally relevant but culturally German. The US industrial reference is Inductive Automation Ignition, not Siemens. Two geos, two narratives, one platform. Decide this now before field-sales creates conflicting messaging on its own.
