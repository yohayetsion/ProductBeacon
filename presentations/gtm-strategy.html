1. GTM Approach: Founder-Led Content-to-Trust Motion

The Motion

ProductBeacon's GTM is founder-led sales, fueled by content-driven inbound and referral activation. This is not PLG (we sell services, not software). This is not enterprise sales (no BDR team, no 6-month cycles). This is a trust-based motion where Yohay's credibility, visible proof of work, and network convert into engagements.

Why This Motion

The logic chain:

  1. Services businesses sell trust. A startup CEO hiring a fractional CPO is making a deeply personal bet. They need to believe this person understands their world, has done it before, and will deliver. No amount of ad spend replaces that.
  1. Yohay's profile IS the differentiator. 17 years at NICE and Cognyte, $200M+ portfolios, 30+ person orgs, a published book on product org management, and an open-source AI system with 82 agents. This profile doesn't need to be manufactured — it needs to be made visible.
  1. AI augmentation changes the unit economics. Traditional fractional CPOs sell time. ProductBeacon sells outcomes amplified by AI. This means the content strategy can show, not just tell — live demos, real deliverables, open-source code. That's a moat competitors can't replicate quickly.
  1. The buyer journey is short but trust-intensive. A Seed-B founder sees a LinkedIn post or hears Yohay on a podcast. They check the profile, read a case study, explore the OS. Within days they're on a discovery call. The GTM motion must compress trust-building into that window.

Why NOT Other Motions

MotionWhy Not
Product-Led GrowthNo self-serve product to adopt. Product Org OS is a lead magnet, not the revenue engine.
Enterprise SalesToo heavy. Requires BDRs, AEs, SEs, and long cycles. ProductBeacon is a one-person-plus-AI operation.
Channel/ResellerPremature. No brand equity yet to attract channel partners at scale.
Paid-firstHigh CAC for a services business with [TBD] price points. Paid is Phase 3, not Phase 1.
Outbound-firstCold email for fractional CPO services has poor conversion. Trust must precede the ask.

The Flywheel

Content (proof of work)
    → Visibility (LinkedIn, speaking, OS)
        → Inbound interest (DMs, referrals, website)
            → Discovery call
                → Engagement
                    → Results (new proof of work)
                        → Content (case study, teardown)
                            → [repeat]

Every engagement produces new proof of work, which becomes new content, which generates new leads. This is the core engine.

Tier 1: High Impact, Start Immediately

2a. LinkedIn — Personal Brand Engine (PRIORITY #1)

Why #1: ProductBeacon's primary buyer (startup CEO/CTO) lives on LinkedIn. Yohay's personal profile is the brand. The company page amplifies; the personal page converts.

Personal Profile Strategy:

  • Positioning line: "AI-Augmented Product Leadership | Fractional CPO | Built 82 AI agents for product orgs"
  • Content mix: 70% proof-of-work (real deliverables, lessons, frameworks), 20% industry commentary, 10% personal/founder journey
  • Posting cadence: 3-4x/week (see Content Strategy below)
  • Engagement: Comment on VC posts, startup founder posts, product leadership discussions. 15-20 min/day.
  • Featured section: AXIA case study, Product Org OS, book, productbeacon.agency link
  • Secondary to personal profile
  • Repurpose personal posts with slight reframing
  • Showcase client work (anonymized if needed)
  • Job-post-style "engagement openings" when seeking new clients
  • Optimize personal LinkedIn headline and About section for ProductBeacon positioning
  • Create a LinkedIn-native version of the AXIA case study (carousel or article)
  • Build a "Featured" section with proof assets
  • Identify and engage with 50 VCs, accelerator partners, and startup founders weekly

2b. Network & Referral Activation (PRIORITY #2)

Why #2: In founder-led services, warm introductions convert at 5-10x the rate of cold outreach. Yohay's 17-year career is an untapped network.

2e. Product Org OS as Lead Generation

Why this works: Open-source tools create goodwill, demonstrate capability, and attract exactly the right audience. Someone exploring "AI for product management" who finds 82 agents and 101 skills is a pre-qualified lead.

Strategy:

  • GitHub presence: Clean README, clear value prop, "Built by ProductBeacon" attribution
  • Content bridge: Every OS update is a LinkedIn post. "Just shipped skill #102: /growth-model. Here's how it works..."
  • Lead capture: OS documentation links to productbeacon.agency. Include a "Want this for your team?" CTA in the README.
  • Community: Respond to GitHub issues, accept PRs, build contributors. Community = trust.
  • Not a product: The OS is a demonstration of methodology, not a product to monetize. Never gate it. Never charge for it. The consulting engagement is the product.

2f. Cold Outreach (Phase 2-3, Selective)

When: Only after content engine is running and there's a warm brand to reference. Discovery Call Structure (30 m...

Discovery Call Structure (30 minutes)

PhaseTimePurpose
Context5 minUnderstand their company, stage, funding, team size
Pain10 minWhat's the specific product challenge? What have they tried? What's the cost of not solving it?
Vision5 minWhere do they want to be in 6-12 months? What does success look like?

Critical rule: Never pitch on the discovery call. Diagnose. The pitch comes in the proposal, backed by the diagnosis.

ProductBeacon
Proposal Format

Proposal Format

Every proposal follows a consistent structure:

  1. Situation Summary — What we discussed, what the challenge is (proves listening)
  2. Recommended Approach — Which service tier fits, what the engagement looks like
  3. Scope & Deliverables — Specific outputs they'll receive (using Vision to Value phases)
  4. Timeline — Phased plan with milestones
  5. Investment — Pricing per the relevant tier
  6. Why ProductBeacon — Brief proof points (AXIA metrics, OS, experience)
  7. Next Steps — Sign, kick off date, onboarding

ProductBeacon
Contract Principles

Contract Principles

  • Simple agreement: 2-3 pages. Scope, timeline, fees, IP, termination.
  • Monthly billing: Invoice at start of each month. Net 14 days.
  • Minimum commitment: 3 months for fractional leadership (meaningful results take time). Month-to-month for advisory.
  • Exit clause: Either party, 30 days notice. No lock-in beyond 30 days.
  • IP: Client owns all deliverables. ProductBeacon retains methodology and AI tools.
