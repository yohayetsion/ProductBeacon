Cybersecurity

Keepnet

Multi-vector phishing simulation across six attack channels in a $5B+ market dominated by email-only incumbents. The product has genuine differentiation; the positioning tries to own too many categories at once.

Market Context

The Security Awareness Training market has evolved from a compliance checkbox into a strategic cybersecurity discipline. The global market sits in the range of $5-6 billion as of 2025, with double-digit annual growth driven by three converging forces: the explosion of AI-generated phishing, regulatory pressure expanding into every sector, and the growing executive consensus that human error, not infrastructure, is the primary attack vector.

The market is also undergoing a definitional shift. Leading vendors no longer sell "training" but "Human Risk Management," reflecting a move from periodic awareness campaigns toward continuous, measurable behavioral change. For Keepnet, this shift creates both opportunity (the category is being redefined) and risk (well-funded players are moving fast to own the new narrative).

Competitive Landscape

KnowBe4 dominates through sheer scale (70,000+ orgs, ~$4.6B acquisition) and an aggressive land-and-expand model with free phishing tests as lead gen. Hoxhunt has carved a credible enterprise niche through gamification, earning genuinely strong outcome metrics (16x lower failure rates). Cofense occupies a distinct lane focusing on post-delivery phishing detection and 8-minute remediation.

Keepnet at Seed stage with ~40 employees is competing against these resources. But it has something none of them have: multi-vector simulation across six channels.

What makes Keepnet defensible

  • Multi-vector simulation breadth. Email, SMS, voice, QR code, MFA fatigue, and callback phishing in a single platform. Real-world attackers have moved beyond email; most competitors have not.
  • "Direct Email Creation" architecture. Guaranteed inbox delivery without whitelisting. This is both an operational advantage (no IT tickets) and a fidelity advantage (simulations match real phishing).

Positioning & Messaging

A- Believability. Enterprise-grade social proof (Vodafone, Coca-Cola, UNICEF, Merck) and specific outcome metrics (90% risk reduction, 168x faster phishing response, 113K hours saved at Vodafone). Gartner 4.9 and G2 4.8 ratings reinforce credibility.

B+ Clarity. The headline communicates autonomous security awareness training clearly. But the "xHRM" acronym introduces unnecessary cognitive load. Prospects must decode both the abbreviation and the "Extended" qualifier.

B Differentiation. Keepnet is making a genuine attempt to carve out territory. The Intelligent Segmentation framework (Risky Behaviors, At-Risk Profiles, Psychological Personas, Cultural Profiles) is genuinely distinctive. But terms like "Hyper-Personalization" and "AI-Powered" have become table stakes language.

The core tension: KnowBe4 tells a platform story. Hoxhunt tells an engagement story. Cofense tells a detection story. Keepnet has the product ingredients to tell a precision story, but currently mixes too many messages without committing to one dominant narrative.

Strategic Recommendations

1. Own the "simulation fidelity" narrative

Rather than competing on template counts or outcome percentages, own the concept that multi-vector, no-whitelisting simulation is the only way to genuinely prepare employees for modern social engineering. This reframes competitors as providing "training theater" while Keepnet provides reality.

2. Make multi-vector simulation demonstrable in the sales process

A live demo that shows an SMS, voice call, and email phishing attempt hitting a prospect's phone and inbox simultaneously would be a visceral, memorable differentiator that no competitor can currently match. The product has the capability; the go-to-market does not yet exploit it.

We identified two additional strategic opportunities around packaging structure and proof narrative depth that require deeper analysis of Keepnet's current sales motion and channel strategy.
